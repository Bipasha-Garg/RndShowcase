How to read the visualization

1. The concentric rings represent different subspaces chosen on the basis of increasing variance of coordinates (outermost ring subspace having the highest variance).
2. The sectors in each ring represent the quadrants in that dimension of subspace.
3. The color of the quadrant represents a positive or negative bit (Blue for positive; Pink for negative).
4. The color of the point connections shows the label (mentioned in the legend).
5. The connected point is basically the same point coordinates in different subspaces.
6. The point of that subspace is placed in its respective sector in the respective subspace ring according to the bit vector.
7. However, the point placement position in its sector is fixed to be in the center.
